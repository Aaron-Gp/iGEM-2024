Project Description

    In human industrial production, agricultural activities and daily life, a large number of harmful chemicals are discharged into the environment directly or indirectly due to inadequate understanding or improper management. Many physiological diseases of humans and wildlife are related to these chemicals. Therefore, it is necessary to fully evaluate the biological effects and toxicity of newly developed chemicals. Oxidative damage and endocrine disruption are two important toxic effects, which have severe effects on biological growth and development, reproduction and immune response. The existing animal or cell test evaluation methods need to be carried out in the laboratory, which has a long cycle and complex operation. The BIT team will use the recA promoter of the SOS family and hormone-sensitive inteins to design a variety of logic gates to build a gated microbial sensor for rapid screening and assessment of multiple toxic effects of pollutants to assist in the assessment of unknown chemicals or pollution risk monitoring of target water sources.
